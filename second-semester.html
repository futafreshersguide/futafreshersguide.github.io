⚠️ Very Important: The CCMAS Upgrade

Before we even talk about courses, you need to know that FUTA is changing its system from the old BMAS to the new CCMAS (Core Curriculum and Minimum Academic Standards).

What this means for you:

Some course codes or the number of units might have changed from what we (your seniors) used to do.

What you should do:

Don't just blindly copy a senior's timetable. Look at the Course Title. Ask your class reps to confirm everything before you register or buy textbooks.

THE STRATEGIC APPROACH: U-H-P & P.A.S.S

To make this semester easy, you need a plan. Before you open a single textbook, use these methods:

🧠 The U-H-P Method

  • U - Understand Yourself: Know your strengths and limitations. What can you do? How many hours can you actually study? Which study techniques work for you? Are you a night reader or day reader?
  • H - Have a Goal: What CGPA do you want to achieve? What should you focus on?
  • P - Plan: Proper planning is like having a map. A good plan means:
    Purposeful (Have a clear target)
    Logical (Based on sound thinking)
    Actionable (Lead to real actions)
    Navigable (Help you navigate challenges)

📚 The P.A.S.S Method

  • P - Past Questions: FUTA lecturers sometimes repeat past questions. Start solving them early.
  • A - Attend Classes & Practicals: Do not joke with attendance. Practicals are free marks but can fail you if missed.
  • S - Start Early: Don't wait until two weeks before exams.
  • S - Sleep & Socialize: You are human. Rest well and keep good friends.

"Remember: Effective planning is the foundation for turning your goals into reality."

I believe we've learned something. Let's apply these principles and make this semester a success!

How to Plan Effectively for This Semester

Effective planning is essential for a successful semester. It involves critical thinking, keen observation, and strategic intelligence to consider various factors and potential challenges ("what if" scenarios). As students, we engage in multiple activities ; classes, practicals, assignments, extracurriculars, and for some, student politics.

📅 Pro Tip

To cover all semester topics thoroughly, it is very easy; all you need is consistency, focus, and seriousness. You can try and list out your courses and topics then dedicate the number of days you want to use to complete them. Then you are all set.

Our main goal is to achieve good grades, which requires more than just average effort; careful planning and dedication are key. Equally important is incorporating breaks and extracurricular activities to prevent burnout and maintain overall well-being.

Balancing Academic and Extracurricular Activities

Mastering the skills of balancing responsibilities, prioritizing tasks, and achieving goals is vital. Creating a detailed schedule that allocates specific times for classes, studying, assignments, and extracurriculars helps ensure all areas are adequately covered and conflicts are minimized.

Setting boundaries is equally crucial. Students should learn to say "no" to non-essential activities that could interfere with academic priorities, such as declining social invitations or taking on too many extracurricular roles.

The Eisenhower Matrix

🔴 Urgent & Important

Do these first: deadlines, emergencies, crisis situations

🟡 Important but Not Urgent

Schedule these: long-term goals, planning, relationship building

🟢 Urgent but Not Important

Delegate if possible: interruptions, some calls, some emails

⚪ Not Urgent or Important

Eliminate or limit the time wasters, mindless scrolling

SMART Goals

  • Specific - Clear and well-defined
  • Measurable - Track your progress
  • Achievable - Realistic and attainable
  • Relevant - Aligned with your objectives
  • Time-bound - Has a deadline

Track your progress, make adjustments as needed, and celebrate small victories to stay motivated.

How to Read and Remember Easily ; The READ RECALL Method

The most effective approach I recommend is the READ RECALL technique:

R

Read Actively

Engage with the material: ask questions, make connections, think critically.

E

Engage with Material

Take notes, highlight key points, summarize main ideas.

A

Associate & Connect

Link new information with existing knowledge and experiences.

D

Digest & Reflect

Pause to think about what you've read; ask "What did I learn?"

R

Review & Recite

Regularly revisit the material and recite key points aloud.

E

Encode & Organize

Use mnemonics, diagrams, or outlines to structure information.

C

Consolidate & Connect

Integrate new info with what you already know.

A

Active Recall

Test yourself without looking at the material.

L

Link to Long-term Memory

Use spaced repetition to transfer knowledge.

L

Leverage Teaching

Teach the material to someone else to deepen understanding.

"Don't underestimate these 10 READ RECALL tips;they are powerful tools for effective learning."
